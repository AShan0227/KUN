# KUN Intellectual Property Policy

This document explains the repository's IP posture in plain language. It is not
legal advice. For enforcement, contracts, patents, trademarks, or company-level
IP strategy, consult a qualified lawyer.

## Current Posture

- GitHub visibility: public.
- License: proprietary source-available.
- Commercial use: prohibited unless separately authorized in writing.
- AI training / fine-tuning / distillation use: prohibited unless separately
  authorized in writing.
- Contributions: accepted only under the contribution grant in `CONTRIBUTING.md`
  and `LICENSE`.

## What Public Visibility Means

Public visibility lets people view the repository on GitHub. It does not make
KUN open source and does not grant permission to reuse the code or product
designs.

GitHub's own licensing guidance says that a public repository needs a license
to be truly open source; without a license, default copyright rules apply and
the owner keeps rights. KUN makes that explicit with a proprietary license.

## What This Protects Well

The current repository policy gives clear notice that others may not legally:

- copy or redistribute the code;
- use the project commercially;
- host KUN as a service;
- train models on KUN materials;
- remove notices or present the work as their own;
- reuse substantial architecture, prompts, workflows, tests, and documents as a
  competing product.

## What This Does Not Fully Protect

Be realistic:

- copyright protects expression, not broad ideas;
- public repositories can still be viewed and technically copied by bad actors;
- product concepts may need trade-secret controls, contracts, patents, or
  trademark filings for stronger protection;
- if a document is truly sensitive, the safest protection is not to publish it.

## Practical Rules For KUN

1. Keep public materials legally marked as proprietary.
2. Do not add MIT, Apache, GPL, BSD, or other open-source licenses to KUN-owned
   code unless the business decision changes.
3. Move highly sensitive product strategy, customer data, secrets, pricing,
   investor material, and unreleased go-to-market details to a private repo or
   private document store.
4. Keep `LICENSE`, `NOTICE`, `COMMERCIAL_USE.md`, `CONTRIBUTING.md`, and
   `SECURITY.md` present in the repository.
5. Keep delivery honesty: do not claim an IP, security, or commercial protection
   is stronger than it really is.
